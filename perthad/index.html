    Perth (IPPH)

    Positions

    Name Callsign Frequency Login ID
    Perth ADC Perth Tower 127.400 IPPH_TWR
    Perth SMC Perth Ground 121.700 IPPH_GND
    Perth ACD Perth Delivery 118.550 IPPH_DEL

    Non-Standard Positions

    Non-standard positions may only be opened during events.

    Airspace

    PH ADC is not responsible for any airspace by default.

    Taxiway Restrictions

    • Link 1, 2, 3 & 4 are limited to Class C aircraft and above
    • For A380/B747 available routes refer to PH Ground Movement Chart - Page 1

    Workload Management

    Pushback Requests on ACD

    During busy periods such as during events, SMC and ACD may coordinate to implement the Pushback Requests on ACD procedure to balance the workload across ACD and SMC frequencies.

    When implementing the procedure, there should be some sort of indication to inform pilots the correct frequency on which to request pushback.

    Local Procedures

    Scenic Flights

    When traffic permits, VFR scenic flights should be cleared via the NORTHCOAST SCENIC (FACT-REEL-DOWN) or SOUTHCOAST SCENIC (FACT-STAD-GUAR-PENL) routes. Coordination with PH TCU is required prior to issuing this clearance, see ACD to PH TCU

    Helicopter Operations

    There are no helipad facilities at IPPH. Helicopters should be issued an airways clearance in accordance with the fixed-wing operation applicable to their flight rules (i.e. SID for IFR helicopters, Visual Departure via appropriate VFR route if necessary for VFR helicopters). Helicopters should be cleared to takeoff/land from runways or taxiways, as deemed appropriate by ADC. The point of takeoff/landing must be specified by ADC.

    Phraseology

    PH ADC: "MRT, taxiway charlie, cleared for takeoff"
    PH ADC: "HWO, runway 33, cleared to land"

    Runway Modes

    Preferred Runway Modes

    Subject to wind limitations, single runway operations may be necessary. However, pending wind limitations (Crosswind <20kts, Tailwind <5kts), the following runway modes are to be used:

    Mode Arrivals Departures
    Northwest Plan (29AD/33A) 29 & 33 29
    Southeast Plan (11AD/15D) 11 11 & 15

    Circuit Training

    Circuit training traffic shall be issued SSR code and clearance to operate within the circuit area not above A015. Circuit training is typically conducted on Runway 11/29.

    Circuit direction should be influenced by the current runway mode currently active. Should single runway operations be in use, circuit direction should conform to the standard (left-hand circuits).

    Mode Circuit Direction
    Northwest Plan Right-hand circuits
    Southeast Plan Left-hand circuits

    SID Selection

    Aircraft planned via HONDA and CAMEL shall be assigned the appropriate Procedural SID that terminates at the appropriate waypoint. Aircraft not planned via any of these waypoints or aircraft with no filed route shall be assigned the Radar SID.

    Off Mode Departures

    Definition

    Off Mode: Aircraft departing from a runway not prescribed as active for departures on the ATIS. For example, a heavy aircraft that operationally requires Runway 11/29 during 15/33 single runway operations due to wind limitations.

    For aircraft departing Runway 11/29 Off Mode, the Radar SID must be assigned.

    Note

    Assigning the Radar SID to an off-mode departure does not meet the requirements for auto release. These aircraft must be 'Next' coordinated with PH TCU.

    Coordination

    Auto Release

    Next coordination is not required for aircraft that are:

    • Departing from a runway nominated on the ATIS; and
    • Assigned the standard assignable level; and
    • Assigned a Procedural SID

    All other aircraft require a 'Next' call to PH TCU.

    The Standard Assignable level from PH ADC to PH TCU is:

    Aircraft Level
    All The lower of A020 and RFL

    Departures Controller

    When a TCU controller is online, aircraft shall be issued with a departure frequency during their airways clearance in accordance with the table below. If no TCU controllers are online, the most appropriate Enroute controller or Advisory frequency shall be issued.

    Runway Via Departure Frequency
    All All 118.7 (PHD)

    ACD to TCU

    The controller assuming responsibility of ACD shall give heads-up coordination to the relevant PH TCU controller prior to the issue of the following clearances:

    • VFR departures into PH TCU CTA
    • Aircraft using a runway not on the ATIS
